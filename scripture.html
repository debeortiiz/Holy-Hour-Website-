What Is Scripture Meditation?

Scripture meditation is the practice of reading the Bible slowly and prayerfully, allowing God's Word to speak to your heart. This ancient Christian practice — sometimes called Lectio Divina (sacred reading) — has been used by believers across all traditions for centuries.

How to Practice Lectio Divina

Four Simple Steps

  1. Read (Lectio): Choose a short Scripture passage and read it slowly, twice or three times, listening for a word or phrase that stands out.
  2. Reflect (Meditatio): Sit quietly with that word or phrase. Let it sink in. Ask: "What is God saying to me through this?"
  3. Respond (Oratio): Speak to God honestly in prayer — with gratitude, questions, or requests — as a natural response to what you've heard.
  4. Rest (Contemplatio): Simply rest in God's presence. Let go of words and simply be with him.

Suggested Passages to Begin

Psalms

"He makes me lie down in green pastures, he leads me beside quiet waters, he refreshes my soul." — Psalm 23:2–3

The Gospels

"Come to me, all you who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest." — Matthew 11:28

The Epistles

"Do not be anxious about anything, but in every situation, by prayer and petition, with thanksgiving, present your requests to God." — Philippians 4:6
